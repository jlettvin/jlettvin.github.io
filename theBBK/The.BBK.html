Slide 1
If we have AI, why do we need a Brain Building Kit?

  • AI is the sizzle. Synthetic Reflexes is the steak
  • The sizzle of intelligence is seductive and well-funded BUT
  • reflexes are what keep everything running
  • All we do is reflex (both The BBK and actual brains)

Slide 2
Why Are Nervous Systems Hard?

  • No simple model neurons exist
  • AI models speculations about complex functions
  • So do HTM, neural nets, and neuromorphic engineering
  • All successfully solve high-value problems BUT
  • All rely on the old limited cybernetic model neuron

Slide 3
Novel IP developed over 16 years.

  • Modeling reflexes is easier than modeling intelligence
  • The BBK is an elementary remodeling of sensorimotor systems
  • It emulates materials from nano to macro scale
  • Simple discrete assemblable elements emerged
    • Motor organs like skeletal and mollusc muscles
    • Sensor organs like eyes, ears, skin, and chemical
    • Interneuron organs to transform sensory to motor
  • Operations appear to have economies similar to in-vivo
    • The design has naturally efficient operations (low energy)
    • The design has natural redundant recovery from injury
  • Discrete component internals resemble those of living organs
  • Simple demonstration reflex systems are being planned
  • If you build a thing, you might understand it

Slide 4
Where can The BBK be deployed?

  • Robots can have fine complex life-like reflexes
  • Processes can respond to conditions without intervention
  • Avatars can respond to game players like living creatures
  • All this without a line of customizing code
  • Individual discrete components have strategic value
    • Heavy lift high power synthetic muscles
    • Highly integrated 3D camera with proximity alarm
  • Valuable new insights for clinical neurologists
    • Confirmable speculations on in-vivo mechanisms
    • Identifiable lesion-to-symptom prediction
    • Retrainable optional pathway identification

Slide 9
Fine Sensorimotor Reflexes

  • Proprioception (postural self-awareness) is natural
  • Nociception (noxious stimulus awareness) is natural
  • Coordination follows transform rules native to neural organs
  • Both sensing and motion achieve "sub-voxel" refinement
  • The whole point is to make it all feel natural

Slide 10

  • We do not hope to compete with AI

Basics of IP Principles

  • In vivo neurons process more information than cybernetic neurons
  • Neural organs have more knowable organization than usually thought
  • Muscles have simple principles of operation
  • Sensory organs do much more than usually thought
  • Only principles found in living animals are used
